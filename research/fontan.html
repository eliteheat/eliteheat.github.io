Real-time shape optimization of the total cavopulmonary connection for patient-specific Fontan surgical planning via hyper-reduced order models.

Overview

The Fontan procedure is a palliative surgery performed in children born with single-ventricle congenital heart defects, creating a total cavopulmonary connection (TCPC) that reroutes deoxygenated blood directly to the lungs. The geometry of the TCPC — how the vessels are connected and positioned — profoundly affects hemodynamic efficiency and long-term outcomes for these patients.

My research develops a real-time shape optimization framework for the TCPC using hyper-reduced order models (HROMs) and free-form deformation (FFD). This enables surgeons to virtually explore and optimize surgical configurations for each individual patient before ever entering the operating room.

This work involves active collaboration with pediatric cardiac surgeons and clinicians at Children's Hospital of Atlanta, Children's Hospital of Philadelphia, and Nationwide Children's Hospital.

Methodology

How it works

01
Patient-Specific Geometry Reconstruction 3D geometrical models of the TCPC are reconstructed from MRI and CT imaging data using segmentation tools including VMTK and Geomagic Studio.
02
Shape Parameterization via Free-Form Deformation A lattice of control points is placed around the TCPC geometry. Deforming these points generates a continuous family of anatomically plausible surgical configurations — the design space for optimization.
03
Snapshot Library & Hyper-Reduction High-fidelity CFD simulations (FEniCS / FreeFem++) are run across the design space to populate a snapshot library. POD-based reduction and empirical interpolation yield a hyper-reduced model that runs in milliseconds.
04
Real-Time Shape Optimization The HROM is coupled with a derivative-free optimizer (Py-BOBYQA) to identify the TCPC shape that minimizes hepatic flow distribution (HFD) maldistribution and power loss — key hemodynamic targets for Fontan outcomes.

Overview of the reduced order modeling framework for accelerated TCPC shape optimization, showing the full pipeline from patient-specific anatomy to optimized surgical configuration.

Clinical Impact

Currently, surgical decisions for Fontan patients rely primarily on surgeon experience and intuition. Our framework provides, for the first time, a computationally tractable method for quantitatively optimizing the surgical plan for each individual patient — with results delivered in a clinically actionable timeframe.

The framework has been validated against full-order CFD simulations and applied to real patient cases in collaboration with surgeons at three major pediatric cardiac centers. Manuscript preparation is ongoing.
